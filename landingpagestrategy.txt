A high-conversion landing page for this platform requires clear calls-to-action, accommodating both eager and hesitant visitors, and plenty of trust boosters. Here’s the conversion game plan: Primary CTA – “Get Access/Sign Up”: The primary goal is likely to have coaches sign up for the service (or request a demo if a direct sign-up isn’t open). This CTA should remain consistent and prominent throughout the page. It was first introduced in the hero section as a button, and it should appear again mid-page (maybe after the pain/solution section as a reminder) and in the dedicated CTA section near the bottom. Using a sticky header with a “Sign Up Now” button that follows the user as they scroll can also be effective, provided it’s not too intrusive. The wording of the primary CTA should emphasize what they’re getting, e.g. “Discover Players Now”, “Start Scouting”, or “Request Your Access” rather than a generic “Submit”. If a form is needed (to collect their email, etc.), consider a two-step conversion: click CTA -> show a pop-up form or take to a short dedicated sign-up page. The less friction, the better; for instance, an email and name might suffice to start, especially if offering a free trial or sample access. The key is to make that primary CTA feel like a valuable step towards finding great players, not a chore. Secondary CTAs – Capture the Hesitant Users: Not every coach will be ready to commit on first visit. We need secondary offers that still capture their information or keep them engaged:
“Download a Sample Report” or “See a Free Player Profile”: This is a classic lead magnet. The page can offer a taste of what a scouting report looks like (with real data anonymized or a past example). A button for this could trigger a modal asking for their email (“Enter your work email to receive a sample scouting report on an African prospect”). Coaches who aren’t ready to pay might still be very curious to see the product. Once they download, they’re in our pipeline for follow-up.
“Subscribe to Newsletter/Updates”: A softer CTA for those who aren’t ready to engage deeply. Something like “Not ready to sign up? Stay on top of African talent – join our monthly insights newsletter.” This way, we at least get their email and can nurture them by sending value (e.g. updates on notable new prospects or success stories). Over time, this can convert lurkers into customers. Place this offer maybe in the footer or a small slide-in prompt after some scroll (but ensure it’s not competing with the primary CTA too strongly).
“Contact Us / Schedule a Call”: Some coaches might prefer to speak to a human (especially if it’s a higher-priced subscription or they have specific questions). A secondary CTA could be a link to “Schedule a 15-minute call with our scouting director” or simply a contact form link. This is for those who are interested but need a personal touch or have objections to clear.
